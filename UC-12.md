# Use Case UC-12: Receive Presentation Schedule

**Goal in Context**:  
Inform an author of the finalized presentation time and location for their accepted paper so they can informing plan and prepare for the conference.

**Scope**:  
Conference Management System (CMS)

**Level**:  
User Goal

**Primary Actor**:  
Author

**Secondary Actors**:  
Administrator  
Editor  
CMS Web Interface  
CMS Database  
Notification Service (e.g., email)

**Trigger**:  
The conference schedule is finalized and published by the administrator or editor.

---

## Success End Condition
* The author successfully receives and can view the presentation schedule details for their accepted paper.

## Failed End Condition
* The author does not receive or cannot view the presentation schedule for their accepted paper.

---

## Preconditions
* The author has submitted a paper to the CMS.
* The paper has been accepted.
* The conference schedule has been generated and finalized.
* The CMS schedule publication and notification mechanisms are available.

---

## Main Success Scenario
1. The administrator or editor finalizes the conference presentation schedule.
2. The CMS stores the finalized schedule in the database.
3. The CMS sends a notification to authors of accepted papers indicating that the schedule is available.
4. The author logs into the CMS.
5. The author navigates to their list of submitted papers.
6. The CMS displays the presentation schedule (time and location) for the accepted paper.

---

## Extensions

* **3a**: Notification delivery fails  
    * **3a1**: The CMS logs the notification failure and allows the author to view the schedule upon logging into the CMS.

* **4a**: Author is not authenticated  
    * **4a1**: The CMS prompts the author to log in before displaying presentation schedule details.

* **6a**: Schedule information is unavailable  
    * **6a1**: The CMS displays an error message indicating that the presentation schedule cannot be retrieved at this time.

* **1a**: Conference schedule has not been finalized  
    * **1a1**: The CMS prevents notification and indicates that the schedule is still pending.

* **2a**: CMS database is unavailable  
    * **2a1**: The CMS displays an error message and delays author notification until the schedule is successfully stored.

---

## Related Information
* **Priority**: High  
* **Frequency**: Once per accepted paper  
* **Open Issues**:  
  * Whether schedule changes after publication should trigger automatic re-notification  
  * Whether authors can download or print their presentation schedule
