# Use Case UC-21: Make Final Paper Decision

**Goal in Context**:  
Enable an editor to make and record the final acceptance or rejection decision for a paper after reviews are completed, and notify the author of the outcome.

**Scope**:  
Conference Management System (CMS)

**Level**:  
User Goal

**Primary Actor**:  
Editor

**Secondary Actors**:  
Author  
CMS Web Interface  
CMS Database  
Notification Service (e.g., email)

**Trigger**:  
The editor selects a paper with completed reviews and initiates the decision process.

---

## Success End Condition
* The editor’s final decision (accept or reject) is successfully recorded and communicated to the author.

## Failed End Condition
* The decision is not recorded or communicated, leaving the paper without an official outcome.

---

## Preconditions
* The editor is registered and authenticated in the CMS.
* The paper exists in the CMS.
* Exactly three completed review forms are available for the paper.
* The CMS decision-recording and notification mechanisms are available.

---

## Main Success Scenario
1. The editor logs into the CMS.
2. The editor navigates to the list of submitted papers.
3. The editor selects a paper with completed reviews.
4. The CMS displays the completed review forms for the paper.
5. The editor reviews the submitted evaluations.
6. The editor selects a final decision (accept or reject).
7. The CMS validates that all required reviews are complete.
8. The CMS records the decision in the database.
9. The CMS notifies the author of the final decision.
10. The CMS confirms to the editor that the decision has been successfully recorded.

---

## Extensions

* **7a**: Fewer than three completed reviews exist  
    * **7a1**: The CMS blocks the decision process and informs the editor that additional reviews are required.

* **6a**: Editor cancels the decision action  
    * **6a1**: The CMS abandons the decision process and returns the editor to the paper list without saving.

* **8a**: CMS database is unavailable  
    * **8a1**: The CMS displays an error message indicating that the decision could not be saved.

* **9a**: Notification delivery fails  
    * **9a1**: The CMS logs the failure and allows the author to view the decision upon logging into the CMS.

* **1a**: CMS web server is unavailable  
    * **1a1**: The CMS displays a system error page indicating temporary service unavailability.

---

## Related Information
* **Priority**: High  
* **Frequency**: Once per submitted paper  
* **Open Issues**:  
  * Whether editors can revise decisions after submission  
  * Whether reviewer comments should always be visible to authors with the decision
