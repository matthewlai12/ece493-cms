# Use Case UC-25: Publish Final Conference Schedule

**Goal in Context**:  
Make the finalized conference schedule publicly available and notify all relevant stakeholders so that authors and attendees are informed of session times and locations.

**Scope**:  
Conference Management System (CMS)

**Level**:  
User Goal

**Primary Actor**:  
Administrator

**Secondary Actors**:  
Author  
Attendee  
Editor  
CMS Web Interface  
CMS Database  
Notification Service (e.g., email)

**Trigger**:  
The administrator selects the option to publish the finalized conference schedule.

---

## Success End Condition
* The final conference schedule is published and accessible, and authors and attendees are notified.

## Failed End Condition
* The conference schedule is not published or notifications are not delivered, leaving users uninformed.

---

## Preconditions
* The administrator is registered and authenticated in the CMS.
* A finalized and validated conference schedule exists.
* The CMS publication and notification mechanisms are available.

---

## Main Success Scenario
1. The administrator logs into the CMS.
2. The administrator navigates to the conference schedule management area.
3. The administrator selects the finalized schedule.
4. The administrator chooses the option to publish the schedule.
5. The CMS marks the schedule as the final version.
6. The CMS makes the schedule publicly accessible via the CMS website.
7. The CMS sends notifications to authors and attendees informing them that the schedule has been published.
8. The CMS confirms successful publication to the administrator.

---

## Extensions

* **7a**: Notification delivery fails  
    * **7a1**: The CMS logs the notification failure and allows users to view the schedule directly on the CMS website.

* **6a**: CMS database is unavailable  
    * **6a1**: The CMS displays an error message indicating that the schedule could not be published.

* **4a**: Administrator cancels the publication process  
    * **4a1**: The CMS aborts publication and leaves the schedule unpublished.

* **3a**: Selected schedule is not finalized  
    * **3a1**: The CMS blocks publication and informs the administrator that the schedule must be finalized first.

* **1a**: CMS web server is unavailable  
    * **1a1**: The CMS displays a system error page indicating temporary service unavailability.

---

## Related Information
* **Priority**: High  
* **Frequency**: Once per conference  
* **Open Issues**:  
  * Whether schedule changes after publication should trigger re-notification  
  * Whether older schedules should remain accessible for archival purposes
