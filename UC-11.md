# Use Case UC-11: Receive Paper Decision

**Goal in Context**:  
Inform an author of the final acceptance or rejection decision for a submitted paper once the review process is complete.

**Scope**:  
Conference Management System (CMS)

**Level**:  
User Goal

**Primary Actor**:  
Author

**Secondary Actors**:  
Editor  
CMS Web Interface  
CMS Database  
Notification Service (e.g., email)

**Trigger**:  
The editor records the final decision for a paper after all required reviews are completed.

---

## Success End Condition
* The author successfully receives and can view the final decision (accept or reject) for their paper.

## Failed End Condition
* The author does not receive or cannot view the final decision for the paper.

---

## Preconditions
* The author has submitted a paper to the CMS.
* All required reviews for the paper have been completed.
* The editor has finalized and saved the decision.
* The CMS notification and viewing mechanisms are available.

---

## Main Success Scenario
1. The editor finalizes the acceptance or rejection decision for the paper.
2. The CMS stores the decision in the database.
3. The CMS sends a notification to the author indicating that a decision is available.
4. The author logs into the CMS.
5. The author navigates to their list of submitted papers.
6. The CMS displays the final decision for the selected paper.

---

## Extensions

* **3a**: Notification delivery fails  
    * **3a1**: The CMS logs the notification failure and keeps the decision available for the author to view upon login.

* **4a**: Author is not authenticated  
    * **4a1**: The CMS prompts the author to log in before allowing access to the decision details.

* **6a**: Decision record is unavailable  
    * **6a1**: The CMS displays an error message indicating the decision cannot be retrieved at this time.

* **1a**: Editor attempts to finalize a decision before all reviews are complete  
    * **1a1**: The CMS prevents the action and informs the editor that additional reviews are required.

* **2a**: CMS database is unavailable  
    * **2a1**: The CMS displays an error message and does not notify the author until the decision is successfully stored.

---

## Related Information
* **Priority**: High  
* **Frequency**: Once per submitted paper  
* **Open Issues**:  
  * Whether reviewer comments should be visible to authors with the decision  
  * Whether authors should be able to acknowledge receipt of the decision
